About Us

A Nigerian Non-Governmental and Non- Profit Organisation

The Potter’s Children Global Foundation (PCG), is a non-governmental and non-profit organisation which caters for underprivileged children (education, food, health-care, etc). PCG was founded and registered with the Corporate Affairs Commission in 2017. It all began when two individuals (co-founders) recognised the need to cater for underprivileged children living in the rural communities of Nigeria. This stemmed from the many children they saw hawking on the streets during school hours, the brilliant kids who were dropping out of schools, the cries of indigent parents who could not clear the medical bills of their ailing children, the wails of hungry and homeless children in the rural villages of Gwagwalada, Abuja.

What We Do

A mission to solve a problem

Education Aid

PCG provides free yearly and termly academic scholarships and other educational aids to indigent schooling children. We also enrol out-of-school children such as drop-outs, hawkers, other children engaged in child labour during school hours.

Medical aid

We provide reliefs and palliatives to hospitalised children and their guardians. These include oﬀsetting medical bills of the patients, distribution of toiletries, beverages, snacks, giving words of encouragement, prayers, etc.

Food drives

We have had several other projects such as food drives, e.g. PCG Feed A Child project (a food drive for almajiris who live on the streets), etc.

Relief materials

At several intervals, PCG provides relief materials in form of food, clothing and money to indigent families and children from donations made, and in response to genuine appeals for succour. For example, the 2020 PCG Covid-19 Relief Initiative (through which we gifted palliatives two indigent families in the heat of the pandemic), etc.
